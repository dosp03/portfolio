Meine Kontakte.

Organize your social life.

"Meine Kontakte" is a contact directory app specifically developed for android phones. It features creating new contacts with their full name, their e-mail, a photo, a phone number and custom additional information that can be tailored, saving the contacts alphabetically ordered to a database, searching for specific contacts, viewing, editiing and deleting them.

Project Scope

For my final project in the "Introduction to App Development" module, I conceptualized and brought to life the "Meine Kontakte" contact directory application. To realize this project, I leveraged the Android Studio development environment, employing Java for the core logic and XML for the layout and design aspects. The app seamlessly integrates with a MariaDB database, ensuring efficient data management.

Notably, the app development was achieved without the reliance on external libraries or frameworks, showcasing a comprehensive understanding of the basics of Android app development.

The app was required to simply enable creating, editing, and deleting entries following the CRUD principle. However, in pursuit of an enhanced user experience and a more visually appealing interface, I incorporated additional features.

These enhancements encompassed the integration of an onboarding tutorial, the inclusion of a search bar to facilitate the swift discovery of specific contacts, and the ability to easily add profile photos to contacts.

App Design & Functionality

App Icon & Splashscreen

The Android app "My Contacts" showcases its logo prominently on the smartphone's start screen, set against a dark background for optimal visibility. Furthermore, upon launching the app, a brief 500-millisecond splash screen is displayed, offering users visual feedback during the loading process, particularly beneficial when dealing with substantial database loads, while also reinforcing brand recognition.

Onboarding tutorial

In the main activity, there's a "Plus" button that directs users to the AddNote activity. Here, users can generate new contacts, with mandatory fields including name and telephone number. Additionally, users have the option to include a profile picture, email address, and extra notes in the free note field. If the required fields are left empty, an error is triggered, preventing the contact from being saved.

Home (Main Activity)

The main activity, which serves as the app's home screen, presents users with a comprehensive view of all contacts stored in the database. These contacts are conveniently displayed within a scrollable RecyclerView. Within this overview, users have the flexibility to interact by either selecting specific contacts to access detailed information, initiating the creation of new contacts through the "Plus" button, or utilizing the search bar to locate specific contacts.

Create contacts

The "Plus" button on the main activity navigates the user to the AddNote activity. Here, they can generate new contacts by providing essential information such as a name and phone number. Additionally, they have the option to include a profile picture, email address, and extra notes in the open notes section. If the mandatory fields are left empty, an error occurs, preventing the contact from being saved.

View details

By clicking on their respective entry in the Main Activity, users can access detailed information about a specific contact. From this point, users can conveniently access the Edit Activity for contact details adjustments by simply tapping the violet "Edit" button positioned to the right of the profile picture. Moreover, a scroll bar and profile picture are intelligently tucked away to optimize screen space, especially when dealing with lengthy note text. This design feature ensures a clean and organized user interface.

Edit contacts

In Edit mode, users have the flexibility to make comprehensive changes to contact information, including the option to update the profile picture. Furthermore, the user can delete a contact from this activity when needed. Similar to the AddNote Activity, certain mandatory fields, namely "name" and "phone number," must be completed to successfully save the contact information. This ensures data integrity and accuracy within the app.

Delete contacts (Dialogue)

Upon clicking the red "Delete contact" button, a dialog box will appear, prompting the user to confirm the deletion of the contact. If the user proceeds to click "Delete contact" within the dialog box, the contact will be removed from both the database and the RecyclerView. The RecyclerView is responsible for displaying the contact list in the main activity. This process guarantees that the deleted contact is no longer visible in the overview, while also ensuring that the database remains current and up to date.

Conclusion

Reflection

During the process of developing the "Meine Kontakte" contact directory app, I've acquired valuable insights into Android app development, user experience design, and database integration. This project has provided a solid groundwork for refining my expertise and understanding in this field. As I look back on the development journey, there are several potential improvements to contemplate, including the incorporation of email verification and the integration of a calling feature, as well as enhancing the functionality of the alphabetical scroll. Furthermore, an alphabetical scroll functionality should exist to enhance the user experience.

Future Steps

Now that the app is fully developed, one could contemplate the idea of offering it on app stores. However, it's important to assess the value it would bring to both users and the developer. Given the abundance of similar apps and the robust built-in features of modern smartphones, it may be more beneficial to consider this project as a valuable learning experience rather than a commercially viable product.

Whether as a learning tool or a stepping stone to future projects, "Meine Kontakte" has provided a solid foundation for growth and improvement in the world of app development.
